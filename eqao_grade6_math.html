Place Value & Integers

Number Sense
Lesson 1 — Reading & Writing Large Numbers ▾

We read and write whole numbers up to 1 000 000. Each digit's value depends on its place: ones, tens, hundreds, thousands, ten-thousands, hundred-thousands.

  • Standard form: 473 825
  • Expanded form: 400 000 + 70 000 + 3 000 + 800 + 20 + 5
  • Word form: four hundred seventy-three thousand, eight hundred twenty-five
  • Compare numbers digit by digit from left to right using <, >, or =.
Example: The digit 7 in 572 381 is in the ten-thousands place → value = 70 000.
💡 To compare numbers, line up the digits and start from the leftmost place.
Practice Questions
What is the value of the digit 4 in 645 832?
Which shows 84 306 in expanded form?
Order least to greatest: 45 308   45 830   45 038

Lesson 2 — Integers ▾

Integers include all whole numbers and their opposites: … −3, −2, −1, 0, +1, +2, +3 …

  • On a number line, values increase as you move right.
  • Negative numbers get smaller the further left you go: −10 < −2.
  • Opposite integers are the same distance from 0. The opposite of −5 is +5.
Ordering: −10 < −3 < 0 < +4 < +9
💡 Think of temperature — −10°C is much colder (less) than −3°C.
Practice Questions
Which integer is LEAST?  −5  −12  0  +3
What is the opposite of −7?

Lesson 4 — Order of Operations (BEDMAS) ▾

BEDMAS gives the correct order to solve expressions with multiple operations.

B — Brackets
E — Exponents
D/M — Divide & Multiply (left→right)
A/S — Add & Subtract (left→right)
Solve: 3 + 2 × (8 − 5)²
Brackets: 8 − 5 = 3  →  Exponent: 3² = 9  →  Multiply: 2 × 9 = 18  →  Add: 3 + 18 = 21

Fractions, Decimals & Percents

Number Sense
Lesson 5 — Fractions ▾
  • Equivalent fractions: multiply/divide top AND bottom by the same number.
  • Comparing: find a common denominator, then compare numerators.
  • Add/subtract with like denominators: add numerators, keep denominator.
  • Improper fractions (e.g., 11/4) can be written as mixed numbers (2¾).
Equivalent: 3/4 = 6/8 = 9/12
Add: 2/7 + 3/7 = 5/7
Compare 2/3 vs 3/4: → 8/12 vs 9/12 → 2/3 < 3/4
Practice Questions

3/8 + 5/8 = ?

Lesson 6 — Decimals & Percents ▾
  • Decimals to 3 decimal places (thousandths).
  • × or ÷ by 10, 100, 1000: move the decimal point right or left.
  • Percent = "per hundred". Convert using:
    Decimal → %: multiply by 100  |  % → decimal: divide by 100  |  Fraction → %: (top ÷ bottom) × 100
3/4 → 0.75 → 75%
0.6 → 60%
45% → 0.45
💡 To compare fractions, decimals, and percents — convert them all to decimals first.

Variables & Equations

Algebra
Lesson 8 — Variables, Expressions & Solving Equations ▾
  • A variable (letter) stands for an unknown number.
  • An expression combines variables and numbers: 3n + 5.
  • An equation says two things are equal: 3n + 5 = 20.
  • Use the balance method — do the same operation to both sides.
Solve 2n + 4 = 12:
− 4 from both sides: 2n = 8
÷ 2 on both sides: n = 4
Check: 2(4) + 4 = 12 ✓
💡 Always substitute your answer back in to check it!
Practice Questions

Perimeter & Area

Measurement
Lesson 12 — Area of 2D Shapes ▾
Rectangle: A = l × w
Triangle: A = ½ × b × h
Parallelogram: A = b × h
Perimeter = sum of all sides

Area → square units (cm², m²). Perimeter → linear units (cm, m).

Triangle b=8 cm, h=5 cm → A = ½×8×5 = 20 cm²
Parallelogram b=10 m, h=4 m → A = 10×4 = 40 m²
💡 Height must be PERPENDICULAR to the base — not the slanted side!

Graphs & Statistics

Data
Lesson 14 — Graphs, Mean, Median, Mode & Range ▾
  • Bar graph — compare categories.
  • Broken-line graph — show change over time.
  • Circle graph — parts of a whole; all sectors must total 100%.
Mean = sum ÷ count
Median = middle value (sort first!)
Mode = most frequent
Range = max − min
Data: 3, 7, 7, 9, 14
Mean = 40 ÷ 5 = 8   Median = 7   Mode = 7   Range = 11
💡 Always sort data before finding the median or range.

Probability

Data
Lesson 15 — Theoretical & Experimental Probability ▾
  • Probability = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes.
  • Scale: 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).
  • Theoretical — what math predicts should happen.
  • Experimental — what actually happened in trials.
  • More trials → experimental gets closer to theoretical.
P(event) = favourable ÷ total
Rolling a die: P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2 = 50%
